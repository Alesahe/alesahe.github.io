you enter the double doors of a little museum.

along one wall there are many taxidermied heads of animals - there's an antelope, a rhino, gazelle. there are even more smaller fish and bats pinned, and a full crocodile, lengthwise, staring blankly.

along another wall is a series of light-coloured wooden cabinets with glass doors. more animals, their bones this time are lit up by a surprisingly warm, inviting light. a floor-to-ceiling case to your left has a human, standing upright, looking straight ahead. they look almost alive but don't stare back at you.

on the other side is a plain white marble table. it's clear of most things but a lone fox stares off into the distance on all four legs.

in the middle of the room is a curious brown-gold globe. it's mounted on three dark wooden legs, on a raised pedestal. you don't understand its symbols and inscriptions or how it moves, but slowly revolves, almost unnoticeable.

you look up. on the ceiling, warm lights dance amongst spears, pinned butterflies, starfish, the pelts of cheetahs, kangaroos and golden ornaments throughout all of it.

a plaque at the base reads "the little museum of curiosities"

you wonder who this place belongs to

you notice that amongst the assortment of cabinets, there's an open doorway directly ahead.
