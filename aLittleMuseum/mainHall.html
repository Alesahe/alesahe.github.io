you enter the double doors of a little museum.

along one wall there are many taxidermied heads of animals - there's an antelope, a rhino, gazelle. there are a few more smaller fish and bats pinned.

along another wall is a series of wooden cabinets with glass doors. more animals, their bones this time, lit up by a surprisingly warm, inviting light.

on the other side is a plain white marble table. it's clear of most things but a lone fox stares off into the distance, upright.

in the middle of the room is a curious brown-gold globe. it's mounted on three dark wooden legs, on a raised pedestal. you don't understand its symbols and inscriptions or how to turn it to see the other side.

you look up. warm lights dance amongst spears, pinned butterflies, starfish, the pelts of cheetahs, kangaroos and golden ornaments throughout all of it.
